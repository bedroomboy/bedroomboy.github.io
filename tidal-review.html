  • When The Pawn... - Fiona Apple

Fiona Apple’s debut effort, Tidal, is a smooth, yet aggressive album.

The abundance of the delightful piano work all through the album, together with Apple’s silky, smooth vocal texture and beautiful vocal melodies on tracks like Sullen Girl, Slow Like Honey, Never Is a Promise and Pale September creates a light, but melancholic atmosphere. Apple also occasionally evinces her energetic singing on tracks like the intro, Sleep to Dream, Shadowboxer and Criminal which does very well to reinforce her emotions.

The concept of this album varies as Apple addresses disparate topics like: finding repose in the midst of uncertainties; how she was perceived by people around her due to the tragic incident of her being raped as a child; loss of innocence and more; but majorly about the frivolousness of her partner, her inexorable craving for him and also her disappointment towards him.

The figurative writing on this album is amazing, as it makes the album open to various interpretations, but what devalues it is the sequencing (particularly the placement of Pale September on the tracklist).

Nonetheless, Tidal is a very great breakup album, and I really enjoyed it!
